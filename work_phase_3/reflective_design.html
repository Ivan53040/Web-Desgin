Reflective Design

Reflective design is the third level of design, focusing on the user's ability to reflect on their experiences with a product. It involves creating products that encourage users to think about their interactions and the implications of those interactions. This level of design is about creating a deeper connection between the user and the product, fostering a sense of ownership and responsibility.

The client, Connect Communities Initiative (CCI), has a clear mission:

Cultural diversity matter because communities want to be seen and celebrated on their own terms ‚Äî not through stereotypes or commercial lenses.

Based on the accessibility audit conducted using the IBM Equal Access Accessibility Checker on the behavioural_design.html page, the following three key issues have been identified, along with their impact on users and proposed solutions for the final website implementation.

Issue Identified: A form element is missing a descriptive label.
Relevant WCAG Success Criteria: 3.3.2 Labels or Instructions (A)
Element on your page affected: This issue affects an input field within a form.

Users who rely on screen readers or other assistive technologies will not know the purpose of the input field, making it difficult or impossible to complete the form accurately. Users with cognitive disabilities may also struggle to understand what information is required.

I will add a label element associated with the form input field using the for attribute, ensuring the label text clearly describes the required input.

Issue Identified: The colour contrast ratio between text and its background is too low.
