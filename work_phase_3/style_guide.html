Style Guides

Design Principles

The design principles are based on the work of Don Norman and his three levels of design. The three levels are visceral, behavioural, and reflective. Each level represents a different aspect of the user experience and how users interact with a product or service.

Visceral

Designing for first impressions. Visceral design is about creating a product or service that is visually appealing and that creates a positive first impression.


It includes: Page Layout, Typography and Color Scheme.

Learn more

Behavioural

Desgining for functionality covering key interactive components. Behavioural design is about creating a product or service that is easy to use and that meets the needs of the user.


It includes: Link, Menu, Navigation, Card, Icon, Button, Forms

Learn more

Reflective

Designing for meaning and identity covering how users see themselves in the website. Reflective design is about creating a product or service that is meaningful and that resonates with the user on a deeper level.


It includes: Value Mapping, Imagery and Tone, Language and Expression and Seeing Ourselves.
